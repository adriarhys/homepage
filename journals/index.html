a collection of questions and thoughts about some readings

Laurel Schwulst, My Website is a Shifting House

    what are the bare minimum requirements for something to be considered a website?

    websites are both the subject and an object what else is?

    a website is never complete constantly shifting and evolving just like us.

J.R. Carpenter, A Handmade Web

    how many webpages are "handmade" these days?

    old website aesthetics are still adored but seen less and less why?

    people often don't think about the hard work put into a website like they do a book or movie.

Taeyoon Choi, Hello World!

    how many people truly understnad the technology used daily?

    "The first computers were human."

    life and computers are similar yet life is anything but binary.

Callum Copley, A Friend is writing

    how can something hold both the power of speech and writing?

    what is the cost of fragmenting your personality and self across many many conversations?

    are we ever offline?

Mindy Seu, The Poetry of Tools

    the web is alive because it can die, does this make anything that exsits on the web also alive?

    how can you use html or css in unconverntional ways?

    the web keeps old art alive.

Becca Abbe, The Internet's Back-to-the-Land Movemen

    everything is finite.

    how does one reverse growth, could reversing growth be considered growing in a different way?

    are utopias truly something good?

Frank Chimero, The Good Room

    everyone is tired everyone is over worked and everything is fighting for our attention.

    there are a lack of third places in the world and the ones that are still around are slowly dying out.

    not all needs require a commercial solution.
